Swish 41

20 years. 25,000 shots. Tour every dunk, layup, three-pointer and, of course, fadeaway jumper that made up Dirk Nowitzki’s career

By John Hancock | Editor, Data and Interactives

Published Oct. 17, 2018

    On Wednesday, Oct. 17, a career that began 19 years and more than 25,000 shots ago will start what is likely to be its swan song, as Dirk Nowitzki and the Dallas Mavericks kick off their 2018-19 season.

    Over that time, Dirk has become the embodiement of the Dallas Mavericks, leading the team to two NBA Finals — including the franchise’s first championship in 2011 — and collecing NBA MVP and Finals MVP awards along the way.

    Nowitzki has made the most of his time on the court. Few could have seen that the lanky German with the goofy haircut that was introduced to Mavericks fans in 1998 would go on to become the sixth highest scoring player in NBA history. With a healthy season, Nowitzki may sit a few notches higher on that list before it’s all said and done, as both Wilt Chamberlain (5th) and Michael Jordan (4th) are within reach.

    And while Father Time has caught up with the Big German, slowing his pace up and down the court, it hasn’t tarnished what will be a career long remembered in Dallas. Take some time and explore that career below:

    A rough start

    Feb. 5, 1999 vs. Seattle

    Dirk made his debut on Feb. 5, 1999. It wasn’t exactly a preview of what was to come. In just 16 minutes, Dirk went 0-for-5 from the field, finishing with just two points.

    First field goal

    Feb. 7, 1999 vs. Golden State

    Two nights after he debuted with an 0-for from the field, Dirk finally scored his first field goal, a 16-foot jumper from the right baseline. He finished the night with 16 points.

    Career high

    Dec. 2, 2004 vs. Houston Rockets

    Dirk scored a career-high 53 in a 113-106 win against the Rockets in 2004. He finished the night 15-for-32 from the field and 21-for-22 from the free throw line.

    10,000 points

    Jan. 18, 2005 vs. Washington

    Dirk notched his 10,000 point with a 23-foot jumper against the Washington Wizards, becoming the fifth (and last) Maverick to reach that milestone.

    Game 6, 2006 Western Conf. Semifinals

    May 22, 2006 vs. San Antonio

    In Game 7 of the Western Conference semifinals, Dirk delievered a driving layup against Bruce Bowen and Manu Ginobli, drawing a foul. He nailed the free throw, sending the game to overtime.

    20,000 points

    Jan. 13, 2010 vs. LA Lakers

    Dirk reached 20,000 points with a 13-foot jumper over Los Angeles Lakers’ forward Lamar Odom in 2010. He’s the only Maverick to ever reach the 20,000 point mark.

    Final touch on a title

    June 12, 2011 vs. Miami

    Dirk put the finishing touches on the Mavericks’ first NBA Championship, scoring the team’s final field goal with 31 seconds left in Game 6 of the NBA Finals.

    30,000 points

    March 7, 2017 vs. LA Lakers

    Dirk scored his 30,000th point with a fadeaway jumper from the right baseline over the Lakers’ Larry Nance Jr., becoming the sixth player to join the 30,000 point club.
