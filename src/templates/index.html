Swish 41

Twenty-one years. Over 26,000 shots. Tour every dunk, layup, 3-pointer and, of course, fadeaway jumper that make up Dirk Nowitzki’s career

By John Hancock | Editor, Data and Interactives

Published Jan. 17, 2019

Updated April 9, 2019

    Editor’s note: This story was updated April 9, 2019, after the Mavericks’ 120-109 win against the Phoneix Suns. Nowitzki scored a season-high 30 points on 11-of-31 shooting.

    On Dec. 13, Dirk Nowitzki, the face of the Dallas Mavericks for the past 21 years, returned from offseason ankle surgery to play for the first time in what is likely to be his final season.

    Nowitzki has become the embodiment of the franchise over a career that includes more than 26,000 shot attempts, leading the team to two NBA Finals — including the franchise’s first championship in 2011 — and collecting NBA MVP and Finals MVP awards along the way.

    Nowitzki has made the most of his time on the court. Few could have envisioned the lanky German with the goofy haircut introduced to Mavericks fans in 1998 would go on to become the sixth highest scoring player in NBA history.

    And while Father Time has caught up with the Big German, slowing his pace up and down the court and diminishing his minutes, it hasn’t tarnished what has been a legendary career in not only Dallas, but the entire NBA. Take some time to explore his career below — for instance: two decades-worth of battles vs. the Spurs or his 2011 title run — or keep scrolling to see some of the milestones that define Nowitzki’s legacy.

    A rough start

    Feb. 5, 1999 vs. Seattle

    Nowitzki’s debut wasn’t exactly a preview of what was to come. In just 16 minutes, Dirk went 0-for-5 from the field, finishing with just two points.

    First field goal

    Feb. 7, 1999 vs. Golden State

    Two nights after he debuted with an 0-for from the field, Nowitzki scored his first field goal, a 16-foot jumper from the right baseline. He finished the night with 16 points.

    Career high

    Dec. 2, 2004 vs. Houston Rockets

    Nowitzki scored a career-high 53 in a 113-106 win against the Rockets in 2004. He finished the night 15-for-32 from the field and 21-for-22 from the free throw line.

    10,000 points

    Jan. 18, 2005 vs. Washington

    Nowitzki notched his 10,000 point with a 23-foot jumper against the Washington Wizards, becoming the fifth (and last) Maverick to reach that milestone.

    Game 7, 2006 Western Conf. Semifinals

    May 22, 2006 vs. San Antonio

    In Game 7 of the Western Conference semifinals, Nowitzki delievered a driving layup against Bruce Bowen and Manu Ginobli, drawing a foul. He nailed the free throw, sending the game to overtime.

    20,000 points

    Jan. 13, 2010 vs. LA Lakers

    Nowitzki reached 20,000 points with a 13-foot jumper over Los Angeles Lakers’ forward Lamar Odom in 2010. He’s the only Maverick to ever reach the mark.

    2011 NBA Finals

    May 31, 2011 - June 12, 2011

    Nowitzki led the Dallas Mavericks to the team's first NBA title against a Miami Heat team that included LeBron James, Dwayne Wade and Chris Bosh, defeating the Heat, 4-2. Nowitzki averaged 27 points per game in the series.

    Final touch on a title

    June 12, 2011 vs. Miami

    Nowitzki put the finishing touches on the Mavericks’ first NBA Championship, scoring the team’s final field goal with 31 seconds left in Game 6 of the NBA Finals.

    30,000 points

    March 7, 2017 vs. LA Lakers

    Nowitzki scored his 30,000th point with a fadeaway jumper from the right baseline over the Lakers’ Larry Nance Jr., becoming the sixth NBA player to join the 30,000 point club.

    Passing Wilt

    March 18, 2019 vs. New Orleans Pelicans

    Nowitzki scored his 31,420th point with a fadeaway jumper from the top of the key over the Pelicans’ Kenrich Williams, passing Wilt Chamberlain to become the No. 6 scorer in NBA history.

    Final home game

    April 9, 2019 vs. Phoneix Suns

    Nowitzki saved his best for his last home game at the American Airlines Center, scoring a season-high 30 points on 11-of-31 shooting, including five 3-pointers.
